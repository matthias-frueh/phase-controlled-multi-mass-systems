# Phase-Controlled Multi-Mass Systems  

Measurement-centric investigation of phase-controlled coupled multi-mass systems with focus on statistical properties of the measured normal force signal.

---

## Core Idea

This project studies whether internally driven, phase-controlled multi-mass systems can produce **reproducible, state-dependent changes in the statistics of the measured normal force signal** under controlled conditions.

The focus is not on new physics, but on:

- precise measurement definition  
- separation of dynamics and measurement artefacts  
- statistical detection of small deviations  

---

## Measurement Model

The observable is defined as:

F_N(t) = m g + \sum_{k=1}^{N} m_k \ddot{z}_k(t) + C(x,t)

where:
- m g : static load contribution  
- \sum m_k \ddot{z}_k(t) : internal dynamic contributions  
- C(x,t) : coupling and measurement artefacts  

---

## Research Objective

Detect a **statistically significant state-dependent mean shift** in the measured normal force under controlled excitation.

---

## Methodology

- phase-controlled excitation  
- controlled coupling of multiple dynamic modules  
- residual-based signal decomposition  
- statistical decision framework (α = 0.05)  

---

## Status

- pre-experimental framework established  
- simulation and modeling ongoing  
- experimental validation planned
